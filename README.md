# Learner Ecology

**A student-facing ecological self-regulated learning tool**

Learner Ecology is an open-source web application built with and for undergraduate students from underrepresented communities in Communication Sciences and Disorders (CSD). It operationalizes an ecological extension of self-regulated learning (SRL) theory — centering environmental, somatic, and relational conditions as learnable self-knowledge, and treating variability as information rather than deficit.

> *"Before a student can regulate their learning, they need to know what conditions make regulation possible for them."*

---

## What it does

Standard SRL interventions teach students *strategies*. Learner Ecology builds the prior knowledge that makes strategies useful — what we call **ecological self-knowledge**: awareness of the environmental, bodily, relational, and temporal conditions that support a given student's cognition.

The tool includes five interconnected screens:

| Screen | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Today's check-in** | Somatic and ecological snapshot — body, environment, energy |
| **Reflect** | Open-ended prompts for noticing patterns in motivation and focus |
| **Metacognition** | Building knowledge of cognition over time (Schraw & Dennison, 1994) |
| **My patterns** | Longitudinal visualization of intra-individual variability |
| **Insights** | Synthesis of check-in data with connections to SRL forethought |

## Theoretical framework

Learner Ecology is grounded in five intersecting bodies of scholarship:

- **Ecological SRL** — an extension of Zimmerman's (2000) cyclical model that adds an ecological awareness stage prior to forethought
- **Metacognitive self-knowledge** — Schraw & Dennison (1994); Cho & Shen (2013) SRLPS
- **Community Cultural Wealth** — Yosso (2005); relational and familial capital as learning conditions
- **DisCrit** — Annamma, Connor, & Ferri (2013); variability as an equity construct, not a deficit
- **Somatic attunement** — Hersey (2022); Menakem (2017); the body as a site of knowledge and resistance

The full theoretical framework manuscript is available in [`/docs/ecological-srl-framework.md`](docs/ecological-srl-framework.md).

## Five constructs measured

| Construct | Description | Closest existing scale |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Ecological awareness | Student's ability to name conditions that support or disrupt learning | Novel — no existing validated measure |
| Somatic attunement | Awareness of body signals as relevant to academic functioning | MAIA-2 (Mehling et al., 2018) — adapted |
| Relational learning orientation | How social presence affects motivation and focus | Strayhorn (2019); Yosso/Morgan CCW instrument |
| Metacognitive self-knowledge | Confidence in knowing what works — not just using strategies | MAI (Schraw & Dennison, 1994); SRLPS (Cho & Shen, 2013) |
| Variability self-efficacy | Belief that one's variability is expected and navigable | DisCrit (Annamma et al., 2013); VanDaalen (2021) |

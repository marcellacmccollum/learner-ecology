Metacognition: knowing how you know
Metacognition isn't just about using strategies — it's about understanding why certain conditions help you think. These questions build that self-knowledge over time.
Knowledge of cognition
What you know about how you learn — your patterns, strengths, what throws you off.
Regulation of cognition
Using that self-knowledge to plan, monitor, and adjust — before and during a task.
This week's metacognitive prompts — tap to respond

If I could tell my past self one thing about how I work best, what would it be?
Reflect on a recent learning moment
Think about the last time you studied and it actually went well. What conditions made that possible — and how much of it was within your control?

Why this matters for RISE-UP

Metacognitive self-knowledge — knowing what works for you and why — is the bridge between self-awareness and self-regulation. Standard SRL tools teach strategies. This tool builds the prior knowledge that makes strategies useful.
Grounded in: Schraw & Dennison (1994) · Hammond, Zaretta — Culturally Responsive Teaching and the Brain · Cho & Shen (2013) SRLPS
